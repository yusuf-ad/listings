# Ano Poli Student Apartment

![Ano Poli student apartment with a comfortable workspace, living area, and view toward the neighbourhood's traditional architecture](/images/guides/apartment-ano-poli-thessaloniki-student-housing-2.webp)

The beginning of Ano Poli is a threshold. Below, the commercial centre buzzes with traffic and shoppers. Above, cobbled streets wind between Byzantine walls and houses that predate the twentieth century. The **Ano Poli student apartment** sits exactly at this threshold, in the Kleious building where the modern city ends and the historic upper town begins. It is a private one-bed apartment with a living room, full amenities, and a security deposit of just €250, the lowest in the entire building. For Erasmus students who want the character of Ano Poli without the commute from its higher reaches, the **Ano Poli student apartment** puts you at the precise point where old Thessaloniki meets new.

## The Kleious Location

### At the Edge of Ano Poli

The Kleious building occupies a strategic position. You are at the beginning of the upper town, which means:

- **Downhill to the centre:** 5-10 minutes walking to shops, supermarkets, and Tsimiski Street
- **Uphill to history:** 10-15 minutes walking to the Byzantine walls, Trigoniou Tower, and Eptapirgio
- **Daily essentials:** Supermarket, bakery, and cafes within 50 metres
- **University commute:** Aristotle University and City College within 20 minutes on foot or by bus

### The Walking Commute

One of the genuine pleasures of living at the edge of Ano Poli is the commute. Walking downhill to Aristotle University takes approximately twenty minutes, passing through the Roman Agora, along side streets with small churches and garden courtyards. It is one of the most scenic university commutes in Europe, and most students who try it prefer it to the bus.

## Daily Life in the Ano Poli Student Apartment

### The Morning Routine

The bakery near the Kleious building opens before seven. Koulouri and tyropita cost under two euros. Greek coffee from the local kafeneio, served with a glass of water and unhurried conversation, sets the tone for the day. By the time you reach campus twenty minutes later, you have already walked through two millennia of architecture.

### Cooking and Eating

The full kitchen setup in the Ano Poli student apartment supports daily cooking. The nearby supermarket covers staples, while the weekly laiki agora offers fresh fruit, vegetables, and cheese at neighbourhood prices. For evenings out, Ano Poli's tavernas serve traditional Greek food at some of the lowest prices in the city, €7-9 for a full meal with wine.

### Study and Relaxation

The separation of workspace and living room in the Ano Poli student apartment allows distinct routines within the same flat. Study at the desk, then shift to the sofa for a break. This physical separation helps maintain focus during long study sessions and creates a sense of transition between work and rest.
