## Living on Aristotelous Square

### Your Daily Radius

Living in the bright studio Aristotelous Square means your daily needs exist within a two-minute walking radius. Supermarkets sit on the side streets behind the arcades. Pharmacies, banks, and bakeries are on every adjacent block. The Modiano and Kapani markets, where you can buy fresh fish, vegetables, spices, and cheese at local prices, are a five-minute walk east.

The waterfront promenade begins at the foot of the square. Morning joggers, evening strollers, and weekend cyclists share the four-kilometre path that stretches toward the White Tower. Your morning coffee routine might involve sitting on the marble steps of the square watching the Thermaikos Gulf, something that never quite becomes ordinary, even after months.

### Getting to University

Aristotle University is accessible by bus in approximately twenty minutes from stops near the square. City College is reachable in a similar timeframe. Most students find that the central location reduces transport time compared to apartments in the suburbs, and the direct bus routes from the Egnatia Street hub, a three-minute walk north, make the commute predictable.

### The Social Geography

Aristotelous Square is where Thessaloniki socialises. The cafes under the arcades fill with students between lectures. The Ladadika dining district is a ten-minute walk west. The Valaoritou bar area is ten minutes northeast. Tsimiski Street, the main shopping avenue, runs parallel just one block north. Living here does not require planning your evenings, the evenings happen around you.
