# Terrace Studio Aristotelous Square

![Terrace studio Aristotelous Square with a private terrace overlooking the upper city rooftops at golden hour](/images/guides/terrace-studio-aristotelous-square.webp)

Most student studios in Thessaloniki end at the front door. You study inside, you eat inside, and when you need air, you lean out a window. The **terrace studio Aristotelous Square** breaks that pattern with a private outdoor terrace that overlooks the upper city, a space where your morning coffee, your evening reading, and your weekend conversations with friends happen under open sky. In a Mediterranean climate where outdoor living is not a luxury but a way of life, this detail transforms a studio from accommodation into something closer to a home. The **terrace studio Aristotelous Square** is the premium option in the Aristotelous building, and the terrace is the reason.

## The Terrace: What It Changes

### An Outdoor Room

The private terrace is not a balcony with a railing and a chair. It is a genuine outdoor space with views toward the upper city, Ano Poli, where Ottoman-era houses and Byzantine walls rise above the rooftops. The orientation catches afternoon and evening light, making it ideal for late-day study sessions or unwinding after lectures. In a city where temperatures stay comfortable outdoors from April through November, this is functionally an extra room for eight months of the year.

### What You See

The terrace faces the upper city, which means your view includes some of the oldest architecture in Thessaloniki. The Trigoniou Tower, the Eptapirgio fortress, and the tiled roofs of Ano Poli form the backdrop. At sunset, the stone walls turn golden and the city below begins to light up. This is not the sea view, it is the other view, the one that connects you to the historical layers of the city, and many residents prefer it.

### How Students Use It

- **Morning coffee:** The terrace catches early light, ideal for a quiet start before lectures
- **Afternoon study:** Natural light and fresh air improve focus during long reading sessions
- **Evening socialising:** Enough space for two or three friends sharing a meal or conversation
- **Weekend relaxation:** An outdoor retreat without leaving home

## Location: Aristotelous Square as Your Base

### Daily Life

Living on Aristotelous Square means your daily radius is measured in minutes, not kilometres. Supermarkets, bakeries, pharmacies, and cafes are all within a two-minute walk. The Modiano and Kapani markets are five minutes east. The waterfront promenade begins at the foot of the square. Tsimiski Street, the main shopping avenue, runs one block north.

### University Access

Direct bus routes from Egnatia Street, a three-minute walk from the building, reach Aristotle University in approximately twenty minutes. City College is accessible in a similar timeframe. The central location means you spend less time commuting and more time studying, or sitting on your terrace.

### Evening and Weekend Life

The Ladadika dining district is a ten-minute walk west. The Valaoritou cocktail bar area is ten minutes northeast. The Aristotelous Square cafes are, literally, downstairs. For a student who wants the city at their doorstep without sacrificing a private retreat, the terrace studio Aristotelous Square offers both.
